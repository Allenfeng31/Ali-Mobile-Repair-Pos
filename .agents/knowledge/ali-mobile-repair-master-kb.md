## 3. Core Technical Insights And Blind Spots

### 3.1 Google Pixel Series

Example devices: Pixel 10 Pro XL and newer Pixel Pro / XL models.

Potential risk:

- Many genuine or high-quality Pixel display assemblies include fingerprint-related hardware, but fingerprint function can fail after screen replacement because of hardware pairing, calibration, or firmware handshake behaviour.
- Direct replacement without pre-testing may leave the screen visually working but biometric unlock unreliable.

Ali repair approach:

- Do not blindly glue or seal the phone before testing.
- First perform a non-destructive display and fingerprint communication test.
- If fingerprint communication fails and the model requires paired hardware reuse, use precision thermal transfer techniques to move the already-paired component from the damaged original screen where technically viable.
- Use official or manufacturer-aligned calibration tooling where available.
- Explain to customers that biometric restoration depends on model, part quality, prior damage, and successful calibration.

Safe wording:

- Good: "We test the display and fingerprint function before final sealing."
- Good: "For some Pixel models, paired hardware or calibration may be required."
- Avoid: "Fingerprint is guaranteed to work on every Pixel screen repair."

### 3.2 Apple iPhone Series

Potential risk:

- Cheaper Hard OLED panels can look attractive on price but have less flexibility than Soft OLED and may be more prone to crack damage under pressure or twisting.
- Screen replacement can affect True Tone, part-pairing messages, brightness behaviour, touch quality, and colour accuracy if data transfer or calibration is skipped.

Ali repair approach:

- Prefer original-grade Soft OLED or quality-screen options matched to the customer's budget and device.
- Use a specialist programmer where the model supports it to transfer original display data and preserve True Tone behaviour.
- Test brightness, touch, Face ID proximity behaviour, speaker mesh alignment, and seal fit before final handover.
- Explain panel options clearly: cheaper panels can save money but may trade off durability, colour, or long-term feel.

Safe wording:

- Good: "We use programming tools where supported to preserve features such as True Tone."
- Good: "We explain Soft OLED and budget panel differences before the repair."
- Avoid: "Absolutely no Apple warning will ever appear on every model."
- Avoid: "All replacement screens are original Apple screens" unless the exact part source is confirmed for that job.

### 3.3 Samsung Galaxy S Series

Potential risk:

- Glass-only repairs on curved AMOLED screens are high risk. Heat, separation pressure, or dust can damage the light-emitting layer and cause later purple bleed, green lines, touch faults, or display failure.
- A repair that looks cheaper at first can become more expensive if the OLED layer fails after glass separation.

Ali repair approach:

- Prefer a full display assembly with frame for Galaxy S-series repairs when reliability matters.
- Use appropriate adhesive, thermal transfer material, alignment pressure, and curing process.
- Rebuild the phone to match the factory structure as closely as practical.
- Perform screen, touch, camera, speaker, charging, and seal-fit checks before return.

Safe wording:

- Good: "We rebuild the seal carefully and test the fit after repair."
- Good: "Factory IP rating cannot be guaranteed after any phone has been opened."
- Avoid: "We restore official IP68 waterproof certification" unless a formal pressure-test and certification process exists for that device.

## 4. Hardware Taxonomy

Use these categories consistently in generated content:

- Display Assembly: full screen assembly, OLED/LCD, digitizer, frame-mounted display.
- Flex Cable: power button, volume button, charging port flex, camera flex, flash, microphone, speaker contact assemblies.
- Premium Battery Replacement: quality cell replacement, calibration where supported, safe adhesive removal, post-repair charging and health checks.
- Board-Level Diagnostics: no power, no charge, short circuit, water damage, connector damage, microscope inspection.
- Data-Safe Repair Handling: repair-first diagnostics where possible, avoid unnecessary erase/reset, explain risk before board-level work.

Battery claim safety:

- Good: "We test battery health, charging behaviour, and shutdown symptoms before and after replacement."
- Good: "Where supported and appropriate, calibration or data transfer can reduce battery-health or part-warning issues."
- Avoid: "Every iPhone battery repair will show 100% health with no warning" unless the technician has confirmed the exact model and method.

## 7. Claim Safety Rules

These rules are mandatory:

- Waterproofing: say "seal rebuilt carefully" or "water resistance may be reduced after opening"; do not promise official IP68 restoration.
- Apple warnings: say "reduce", "preserve where supported", or "test before handover"; do not promise zero warnings for every model.
- Original parts: only say "genuine" or "original" when confirmed. Otherwise use "original-grade", "premium", "OEM-standard", or "high-quality".
- Battery health: do not promise 100% health display across all models. Say testing/calibration/data transfer is model-dependent.
- Data: do not promise zero data risk for liquid damage or board-level repair. Say data is handled carefully and customers are warned before risky work.
- Time: do not promise same-day repair if parts availability is unknown. Say "often same-day when parts are in stock."
